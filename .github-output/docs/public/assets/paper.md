# Room-Temperature Superconductivity in a Carbonaceous Sulfur Hydride

> **Original work:** E. Snider, N. Dasenbrock-Gammon, R. McBride, M. Debessai, H. Vindana, K. Vencatasamy, K.V. Lawler, A. Salamat & R.P. Dias. "Room-temperature superconductivity in a carbonaceous sulfur hydride." *Nature* **586**, 373–377 (2020). [DOI:10.1038/s41586-020-2801-z](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-020-2801-z)

> **RETRACTED** by Nature editors on 26 September 2022. [Retraction note](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-022-05294-9)

## Abstract

One of the long-standing challenges in experimental physics is the observation of room-temperature superconductivity. Recently, high-temperature conventional superconductivity in hydrogen-rich materials has been reported in several systems under high pressure. An important discovery leading to room-temperature superconductivity is the pressure-driven disproportionation of hydrogen sulfide (H₂S) to H₃S, with a confirmed transition temperature of 203 K at 155 GPa. By introducing methane at low pressures into the H₂S + H₂ precursor mixture for H₃S, molecular exchange is allowed within a large assemblage of van der Waals solids that are hydrogen-rich with H₂ inclusions. The authors report superconductivity in a photochemically transformed carbonaceous sulfur hydride system, starting from elemental precursors, with a maximum superconducting transition temperature of 287.7 ± 1.2 K (about 15°C) achieved at 267 ± 10 GPa.

## Key Claims

### Experimental Results (as originally reported)
1. **Maximum Tc = 287.7 ± 1.2 K** at 267 ± 10 GPa — room temperature
2. **Superconducting range**: 140 to 275 GPa, with sharp Tc upturn above 220 GPa
3. **Zero resistance** observed at multiple pressures
4. **Magnetic susceptibility** measured up to 190 GPa, showing diamagnetic signal
5. **Magnetic field suppression**: Tc reduced under external magnetic field
6. **Isotope effect**: Carbon-13 substitution showed shift consistent with BCS mechanism
7. **Sample preparation**: Photochemical synthesis from C+S+H₂ elemental precursors in DAC

### Pressure-Tc Relationship
- Below 220 GPa: moderate Tc values (~150-200 K)
- Sharp upturn above 220 GPa reaching 287.7 K at 267 GPa
- Attributed to structural transition in the C-S-H compound

### Evidence Presented
1. **Resistance measurements**: Four-probe in DAC, R→0 at Tc
2. **Magnetic susceptibility**: ac susceptibility showing diamagnetic transition (THIS IS THE PROBLEMATIC DATA)
3. **Magnetic field dependence**: Tc suppression with field
4. **Isotope effect**: ¹³C substitution

## Retraction Details

### Background Subtraction Issues
- The magnetic susceptibility plots in Fig. 2a and Extended Data Fig. 7d used a "non-standard, user-defined" background subtraction procedure
- Details of the procedure were not specified in the paper
- Nature stated these processing issues "undermine confidence in the published magnetic susceptibility data as a whole"

### Van der Marel & Hirsch Analysis (arXiv:2201.07686)
Key findings of independent analysis:
1. **Reversed procedure**: Data compatible with background being ADDED to a pre-determined result, not subtracted from raw measurements
2. **"Measured voltage" data are not raw data**: The supposedly unprocessed measurements appeared artificially constructed
3. **All six pressures pathological**: Background-corrected susceptibility data at all pressures showed statistical anomalies
4. **No valid method found**: Four different proposed data-processing methods evaluated; none could reproduce published results

### Failed Replication Attempts
1. **Eremets group** (Max Planck Institute): Spent 6 months trying to replicate. Observed superconductivity in some CSH samples only up to ~200 K (−73°C), far below room temperature
2. **Goncharov group** (Carnegie Institution): Could not create any CSH compound undergoing structural change at the claimed pressure

### Dias Subsequent Issues
- A later paper by same group (N-doped lutetium hydride, Nature 2023) was also retracted
- Ranga Dias faced investigations for data integrity issues across multiple publications

## Context in Superconductivity Trilogy

This paper builds directly on:
1. H₃S at 203 K (Drozdov et al., Nature 2015) — confirmed, H₃S identified
2. LaH₁₀ at 250 K (Drozdov et al., Nature 2019) — confirmed independently

The C-S-H paper claimed the next step: room temperature. However, unlike papers 1 and 2 (from Eremets group), this paper (from Dias group) has been retracted due to data integrity concerns, specifically around the magnetic susceptibility evidence.
